A timeline of Bernie's life

  • 1941 - Born in Brooklyn, NY
  • 1962 - Leads students in a multi-week sit-in to oppose segregation in off-campus housing
  • 1981 - Elected mayor of Burlington, VT
  • 1984 - Mayor Sanders establishes the Burlington Community Land Trust, the first municipal housing land trust in the country for affordable housing
  • 1990 - Sanders defeats incumbent Rep. Peter Smith in a race to be Vermont’s sole congressman
  • 2001 - Sanders votes against the USA Patriot Act. “All of us want to protect the American people from terrorist attacks, but in a way that does not undermine basic freedoms,” Sanders says at the time.
  • 2010 - President Barack Obama signs into law the Affordable Care Act with a major Sanders provision to expand federally qualified community health centers.
  • 2013 - Sanders, backed by seniors, women, veterans, labor unions and disabled Americans leads a successful effort to stop a “chained-CPI” proposal supported by Congressional Republicans and the Administration to cut Social Security and disabled veterans’ benefits.
  • 2013 - Sanders introduces legislation to break up major Wall Street banks so large that the collapse of one could send the overall economy into a downward spiral.
  • 2014 - A bipartisan $16.5 billion veterans bill written by Sanders, Sen. John McCain and Rep. Jeff Miller is signed into law by President Barack Obama. The measure includes $5 billion for the VA to hire more doctors and health professionals to meet growing demand for care.
  • 2015 - Sanders votes against the Keystone XL pipeline which would allow multinational corporation TransCanada to transport dirty tar sands oil from Canada to the Gulf of Mexico.
  • 2015 - Sanders declares his candidacy for the Democratic nomination for President of the United States.
